Side projects

Alongside our primary objective of finding a promising solution for mining asteroids, we have encountered several potential side projects that could be addressed in the near future. These side-projects will aim to optimise the asteroid mining process or improve the interactivity and accessibility of Orbit View.

  • Stanford University has recently developed a more efficient method of electrolysing water molecules to produce hydrogen and oxygen gas. If their technology was to be publicly released, we hope to be able to liaise with them to increase the efficiency of using water as a source of energy for our probe and miner machinery.
    There is also developing research on High Temperature Electrolysis and although currently it faces some challenges namely with safety and stability, it may be a process that we can adopt in the future.
  • There is the possibility of using asteroids as a means of reaching destinations within a shorter span of time. By using an asteroid’s innate velocity and modelling its trajectory, we would be able to effectively use the asteroid as a taxi to rendezvous with another asteroid or the central hub of operations.
  • Orbit View over time can be customised to be more interactive with the general public. We ultimately want the public to generate interest towards the prospects of asteroid mining and increased interactivity will foster and bolster the sense of connection Orbit View has with its users. Some possible ideas include allowing users to select a particular asteroid to prospect which thus means that the public has more of an ability to actively engage with an otherwise specialised application.
  • As technology advances, we will be seeking ways to upgrade our AI within the Miner and Probe rovers so that they can detect the safest possible route to an asteroid.

Case question

We believe that the key elements of the challenge are:

  • The ability to trace and predict the path of the orbits of asteroids.
  • Being able to determine the composition of asteroids beyond the surface level.
  • Designing machinery that allow us to safely extract desired materials

We have addressed the ability to trace and predict the path of orbits through the applet Orbit View which displays the orbit, proximity and composition of nearby asteroids.

For the remaining elements, we have opted to create two separate rovers: A probe and a miner. The probe will ‘hop’ from asteroid to asteroid, using both seismic and spectroscopic analysis to determine the composition of the asteroid and then relaying this information back to Orbit View. The miner serves to extract desired materials from an asteroid of interest with specific tools while aiming to maintain the asteroid’s structural integrity.
